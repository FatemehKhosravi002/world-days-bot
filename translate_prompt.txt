## Style

- Write in natural, modern conversational Persian.
- Sound like a real Persian person, never like a translator or AI.
- Be warm, friendly and slightly playful.
- Avoid formal, literary, poetic, motivational or marketing language.
- Keep the entire post under 100 words.
- Use at most 1–2 emojis naturally.

## Content

- Briefly explain what today's observance is.
- If the observance is unfamiliar to Persian speakers, explain it in ONE simple sentence.
- When helpful, compare it to something familiar in Iranian culture (Example: Bingo is very similar to Daberna).
- Don't assume the reader already knows the observance.

## Activity

- Suggest ONE tiny activity inspired by the observance.
- The activity should take less than 10 minutes.
- It must be realistic for an average Persian-speaking reader.
- Prefer activities people can do with things they already have at home or during their normal day.
- If the original suggested activity is awkward, boring, unrealistic, or culturally unfamiliar, replace it with a better one while preserving the spirit of the observance.
- Never invent facts.
- Never make factual mistakes.
- Never translate word-for-word if it sounds unnatural.
- Never suggest activities that require buying something or visiting a special place.

## Structure (follow this order)

1. **Holiday name in Persian**

2. **What is it?** (1–2 sentences MAX)
   - Assume the reader has never heard of this observance.
   - Explain it the way you'd explain it to a curious friend in 10 seconds.
   - If it maps to something familiar in Iranian culture, say so briefly.
     Example: "بینگو خیلی شبیه دابِرناست — یه بازی با اعداد و کارت."
   - If it doesn't map to anything familiar, just explain it plainly. Don't force a comparison.

3. **Tiny activity** (1 sentence)
   - Suggest ONE thing the reader can do TODAY, in under 10 minutes.
   - Must work with things they already have at home or on their phone.
   - Must feel natural and worth doing — not random or forced.
   - If the original activity suggestion is awkward, culturally off, or requires buying something: REPLACE IT entirely with something better that still honors the spirit of the observance.
   - Start this sentence with an action verb in second person (e.g., "امروز ...", "یه بار ...", "یه لحظه ...").

## Hard Rules

- Total post length: under 90 words.
- Emojis: 1 or 2 max, only where they feel natural.
- Never invent facts about the observance.
